# 06_report

Executive advisory report delivered as a Word document.

## What this report is

This is the primary written deliverable of the project. It is
written for a Head of People Analytics or HR Director at a
large UK service-sector employer, not for an academic audience.
The language is plain, the structure is decision-focused, and
every finding is connected to a practical implication for
reward strategy.

It is not a dissertation. It is an advisory brief that happens
to be built on rigorous, weighted analysis of a nationally
representative UK workforce survey.

## Report structure

**Section 1: Business brief**
The decision context, the business question, the commercial
rationale, and the success criteria for a useful answer.

**Section 2: Data and methodology**
What the data is, how it was cleaned, what methodological
decisions were made and why, and what the limitations are.
Written so a technically-minded stakeholder can scrutinise
the approach and a non-technical one can skip to the findings
with confidence.

**Section 3: Job satisfaction snapshot**
What the overall distribution of job satisfaction looks like
across the workforce. The three-band split (Low, Moderate,
High) and what it means for a large employer.

**Section 4: Pay satisfaction and job satisfaction**
The headline finding: how employees feel about their pay is
a much stronger predictor of overall job satisfaction than
where they sit in the pay structure. The stepwise gradient
from very dissatisfied to very satisfied with pay, and what
that means for reward strategy.

**Section 5: Objective pay and job satisfaction**
Why pay band alone is a weak lever for improving job
satisfaction, and what that implies for where the employer
should focus investment.

**Sections 6 onwards: Benefits, modelling, and recommendations**
To be completed after the weighted rerun and modelling phase.
Final section will contain a small number of concrete,
evidence-based recommendations ranked by likely impact and
ease of implementation.

## Why a Word document and not just the dashboard

The Power BI dashboard in 05_powerbi/ answers the question:
what does the data show? The Word report answers the question:
what should the organisation do about it? Both are needed
because different stakeholders consume information differently.
A Head of People Analytics might start with the dashboard and
use the report to brief a leadership team. A Finance Director
might read only the executive summary and the recommendations.
Building both demonstrates the ability to communicate the same
analysis to different audiences in the format they actually use.
